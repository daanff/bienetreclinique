Our expertise

Our Services

Acupuncture, massage therapy and complementary TCM treatments delivered with care and professionalism.

Acupuncture

Personalized care with single-use sterile needles, based on traditional Chinese medicine to harmonize body and mind.

Classic acupuncture

Needle insertion on acupuncture points and meridians to relieve chronic pain, muscle tension, stress, insomnia and digestive issues while supporting overall energetic balance.

Facial cosmetic acupuncture

Facial acupuncture to soften wrinkles, even skin tone, reduce pigmentation and restore natural radiance—without surgery or invasive products.

Weight management acupuncture

Acupuncture support for appetite, digestion and metabolism within a holistic approach to rebalancing and healthy lifestyle habits.

Stroke recovery acupuncture

Complementary support for rehabilitation after a stroke (CVA): targeted point stimulation to help support limb mobility, muscle tone and motor function, alongside your medical care. Results vary from person to person.

Facial paralysis acupuncture

Targeted care for facial paralysis (such as Bell’s palsy): fine needles on facial points to help support the recovery of facial muscle mobility, symmetry and tone, alongside your medical care. Results vary from person to person.

Massage therapy

A wide range of manual techniques for relaxation, lymphatic drainage, sports recovery, targeted care or family wellness.

Deep tissue massage

Sustained pressure and deep work on muscles and fascia to release knots, ease persistent stiffness and improve range of motion.

Acupressure massage

Targeted pressure and work along meridians inspired by TCM to harmonize Qi flow, release tension and support the body's self-regulation.

Swedish massage

Long, flowing strokes, kneading and percussion to relax superficial and deep muscles, boost circulation and ease everyday tension.

Relaxation massage

Slow, enveloping movements with light to moderate pressure to release accumulated stress, restore inner calm and support restful sleep.

Lymphatic drainage

Light, slow, rhythmic manual techniques to support lymphatic circulation, ease the feeling of heavy or swollen legs, encourage elimination of metabolic waste and promote deep body relaxation.

Facial massage

Gentle techniques on the face, neck and scalp to relax features, release tension and brighten the complexion.

Prenatal massage

Safe, adapted techniques for each stage of pregnancy to relieve lower back tension, heavy legs and discomfort, in a calm and respectful setting.

Pediatric massage

A gentle, age-appropriate approach to soothe the nervous system, encourage rest and support daily well-being, with a parent present.

Foot reflexology

Precise stimulation of foot reflex zones to induce deep relaxation, support homeostasis and promote an overall sense of balance.

Integrative therapies

Complementary traditional Chinese medicine techniques to enhance and extend the benefits of your treatments.

  • Cupping: decongestion and tension release through suction
  • Moxibustion: gentle heat on acupuncture points to tonify energy
  • Traditional Chinese medicine consultation: personalized assessment of your balance

Chinese patent medicines

Recommendation of patent medicines as concentrated powder or granules (professional dispensary bottles), selected according to your TCM assessment and constitution within personalized follow-up care.

  • Classical formulations in bottles, dosed according to your needs
  • Quality patent granules and powders, dispensed in clinic
  • Guidance on use and complementary follow-up aligned with TCM principles
Coverage

Road & work accidents: SAAQ & CNESST

Were you injured in a road or workplace accident? We welcome clients referred through the SAAQ and the CNESST for acupuncture treatments authorized under your file.

SAAQ

Road accident

Québec’s public automobile insurance board (SAAQ). When your file is open and care is authorized, we provide acupuncture treatments as part of your rehabilitation.

CNESST

Workplace accident

Québec’s workplace health, safety and labour standards board (CNESST). We support referred workers with care authorized under their file.

Bring your file number and treatment authorization; official receipts are provided. Questions about your eligibility? Contact us.

Frequently asked questions

FAQ

Answers to common questions about our treatments, session length and insurance coverage.

Does acupuncture hurt?

We use sterile, single-use needles that are very fine. Most people feel a slight pinch or localized pressure, sometimes brief numbness around the point. The vast majority of patients find the session relaxing and well tolerated.

How long is a first acupuncture appointment?

For a first visit including consultation and acupuncture, allow about 90 minutes. A follow-up classic acupuncture session is usually 60 minutes.

Are treatments covered by insurance?

Depending on your plan, acupuncture (billed by a licensed OAQ acupuncturist), massage therapy and several natural therapy approaches may be eligible for reimbursement under group or private insurance.

Bring your insurance card to your visit; our team can guide you. Questions? Call (450) 486-6677.

What should I do after a massage?

Drink plenty of water, avoid strenuous activity for a few hours and allow time to rest so you can enjoy the full benefits of the treatment. Mild muscle soreness may occur and usually fades within 24 to 48 hours.

Contact us if you experience unusual or persistent discomfort.

Do you accept SAAQ and CNESST clients?

Yes. We welcome people referred by the SAAQ (Québec’s public automobile insurance board) and the CNESST (Québec’s workplace health, safety and labour standards board) for acupuncture treatments authorized under their file.

Please bring your file number, your referral or your treatment authorization.

Another question? Email info@bienetreclinique.ca or call (450) 486-6677.
